Vardi par endcalendar par subsection*textbfSupported Funds begincalendar item[09/17-09/19] ``Multi-Platform, Multi-Architecture Runtime Verification of Autonomous Space Systems'', NASA. endcalendar par iffalse noindenttextbfMajor Research Accomplishment par beginitemize item textbfSafety Hardware Model Checking: SAT-based model checking dominates all other approaches in hardware verification. IC3/PDR is one of the most popular SAT-based model checking approaches off-the-shelve. Although it performs well in practice, IC3/PDR is hard to understand, and essentially a mixture of different heuristics. In my recent work, I present CAR (Complement Approximation Rechability), a new safety model checking framework. Comparing to IC3/PDR, CAR is motivated from the basic reachability analysis, so it is easy to understand and a more flexible framework. We also show that CAR complements IC3/PDR in many cases, even though CAR is not able to beat IC3/PDR right now. This paper is currently under review. Like IC3/PDR, CAR can be improved by applying heuristics, which is one of my long-term goals. par item textbfSAT-based LTL reasoning: Traditional LTL reasoning is based on the tableau construction, which essentially expands LTL formulas syntactically to obtain all the successors of the current formula. The bottleneck is the tableau expansion cost can be exponential in the worst case. The SAT-based LTL reasoning utilizes SAT techniques to expand LTL formulas semantically, and obtains the successors on-the-fly. Although, the worst cost is still exponential, it enables to process further reasoning before all next states are generated. I showed that this new approach performs very well in satisfiability checking problem, and the tool Aalta becomes one of the best LTL satisfiability solvers. The related work was published in HVC 2015. par Many future works are underlaid on this new reasoning framework. For examples, it is able to construct the automata on-the-fly, which results in model checking also on-the-fly. The SAT-based framework is trivial to be adapted to be SMT-based, which then can handle a more expressive reasoning. Furthermore, the techniques can be specialized to the reasoning over finite traces, which allows us to extend the research area to AI. par item textbfLTL Satisfiability Checking: Linear Temporal Logic (LTL) has been widely used as a formal specification language in formal verification area, such as model checking, runtime verification and etc. The satisfiability problem is not only fundamental for LTL, but also a pre-processing step for LTL-based verification. Classical solution for LTL satisfiability checking requires to construct an automaton equivalent to the LTL formula. My major contribution is to propose an on-the-fly (lazy) framework to check LTL satisfiability without generating the whole automata. Related works are published in TIME 2013 and FSE 2014. Moreover, I also adapted the technique to the satisfiability checking of LTL over finite traces, which is now popularly applied to AI. This paper was published in ECAI 2014. par item textbfLTL synthesis over finite traces: Synthesis for LTL over infinite traces requires to construct a deterministic automaton accepting infinite traces and reduce the synthesis to the parity game, which are very complicated in theory and efficientless practically. However, it will be much simpler when we consider LTL over finite traces. In this situation, a DFA can be constructed from the input formula, and then the synthesis is able to be reduced to the DFA game. Our contribution is to go through the whole process, and release such a tool for LTL synthesis over finite traces. This paper is accepted by IJCAI 2017. par From our previous experiments, the DFA construction is still the most consuming part in the synthesis. We aim to figure out an on-the-fly approach for synthesis in the future, which is able to avoid the heavy cost from the DFA construction. par enditemize fi par subsection*textbfTools begincalendar item[2017-] Safety Hardware Model Checker: SimpleCAR
